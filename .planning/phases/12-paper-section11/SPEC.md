## Structure

### Paragraph 1 — What we showed
- LLMs have consistent, measurable geometric structure in how they navigate between concepts
- Consistent with quasimetric geometry (asymmetric, triangle inequality ~91%)
- Populated by bottleneck bridges (not associations)
- Organized into model-specific gaits on a shared geometry
- Holds across 12 models from 11 families and resists protocol variation

### Paragraph 2 — What we learned
- Mechanism ceiling is itself the most important finding
- 7 single-variable hypotheses falsified across Phases 8-10
- Qualitative structure characterizable; quantitative mechanism opaque to simple models
- Parallels broader interpretability challenge: structure detectable before explainable

### Paragraph 3 — What it means
- Static geometry well-studied; navigation is not
- Karkada et al. proved why geometry exists; we showed what it supports
- New evaluation paradigm: not testing what models know, but how they navigate what they know
- 29 dead ends and mechanism ceiling are honest accounting

## Key Numbers
- ~21,540 runs, 12 models, 11 families
- Gait range 0.258-0.747 (2.9x)
- Asymmetry 0.811
- Triangle inequality ~91%
- 7 falsified hypotheses
- 29 dead ends
